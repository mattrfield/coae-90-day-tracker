What is the HTB COAE?

The HTB Certified Offensive AI Expert (COAE) is Hack The Box's professional certification for AI red teaming, launched in April 2026. It validates advanced, hands-on skills in attacking and securing AI/ML systems, and was built in collaboration with Google, aligned to Google's Secure AI Framework (SAIF).

The certification is earned by completing the AI Red Teamer Job-Role Path — 12 modules, 230 sections, rated Hard — and then passing the exam.

The exam

The COAE exam is a 7-day practical engagement: you assess a complex AI-driven infrastructure, then submit a commercial-grade technical report. It tests adversarial ML, LLM prompt injection and jailbreaking, LLM output exploitation, AI application/system security, AI privacy, and AI defense.

HTB recommends the Silver Annual subscription, which includes the full path plus one exam voucher valid for two attempts.

The 12 modules in order

  • Fundamentals of AI · Applications of AI in InfoSec
  • Introduction to Red Teaming AI · Prompt Injection Attacks · LLM Output Attacks
  • AI Data Attacks · Attacking AI - Application and System
  • AI Evasion: Foundations · First-Order Attacks · Sparsity Attacks
  • AI Privacy · AI Defense

How to use this tracker

  • Set your start date at the top — every day's calendar date and your pace indicator are computed from it.
  • Tick each task as you finish it. A day turns green when all its tasks are done.
  • Use the Notes field on each day to journal what you learned, payloads that worked, or things to revisit.
  • The dashboard tracks overall %, days completed, your best streak, and whether you're ahead of or behind pace.
  • All progress is saved automatically in your browser. Use Export backup regularly to keep a copy.
Reality check: finishing a Hard-rated, 230-section path and being exam-ready in 90 days from a foundations start at ~10–20 hrs/week is ambitious. This plan front-loads fundamentals and builds in review and catch-up days. If you fall behind, that's expected — extend the timeline rather than skipping the hands-on labs. Depth beats speed on the practical exam.
